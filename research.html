Research Projects



Mantle Structure beneath South America

from Finite-Frequency Teleseismic S-wave Tomography | Rodríguez, Portner, Beck, et al, in prep, 2019

The Andean subduction zone spans approximately 7,000 km of the western South American margin, where the oceanic Nazca and Antarctic plates are presently subducting eastward beneath the continental South American plate. Previous seismic imaging work has shown that the structure of the Nazca plate exhibits significant variability along strike. Excluding global studies, previous work has focused on small regions of the slab and surrounding mantle at a time. We hope to improve upon this work by inverting for the velocity structure of the subduction zone in a single model, providing a high-resolution image on the continent-scale and the chance to look at the variability within the slab and mantle in its entirety. By using S-wave velocities, which are sensitive to different material properties than P-wave velocities, we hope to better characterize the ongoing processes of the subduction zone, particularly in the mantle.



Responses to Ridge Subduction: Patagonia, Chile

from Teleseismic Receiver Functions | Rodríguez and Russo, Geosphere, 2019
doi:10.1130/GES01692.1

The tectonics of Patagonia are largely dominated by the subduction of the Chile Ridge, the active spreading ridge between the Antarctic and Nazca plates, which intersects the Chile trench forming a trench-trench-ridge triple junction (see figure; Herron et al., 1981). The subduction of the Chile Ridge and its transforms has been associated with diachronously developed variable structure and magmatism of overriding South America, where important differences between morphology and structures are found in fore-arc areas north and south of the present Chile triple junction (e.g., Ramos, 1989; Gorring, et al., 1997). To assess the crustal structure and understand how these tectonic processes affected the region, we combined data from two seismic networks, the Chile Ridge Subduction Project and Seismic Experiment of Aisen Chile - yielding a total of 77 broadband seismic stations - deployed from 2004 to 2007 for receiver function analysis.
